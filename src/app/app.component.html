Maximal lotteries is an attractive randomized voting rule, first considered by Germaine Kreweras in 1965 and independently proposed by Peter C. Fishburn in 1984.

Please see Wikipedia or these presentations (Practical Voting Rules, Maximal Lotteries) for more information.

Other websites for computing maximal lotteries: Pnyx, votation.ovh.

This website was created by Dominik Spies, Christian Stricker, Florian Brandl, and Felix Brandt and is maintained by the Chair for Decision Sciences & Systems at Technische Universität München.
